Mental Health Resource Guide

What Is Mental Health?

How to use a framework effectively

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. Our mental health affects how we think, feel, and even act. It helps determine how we handle stress, interact and relate with others, and make choices. Mental health is important all the way from childhood through adulthood.

Many factors influence mental health. These factors are biological - such as genes or brain chemistry, life experiences - such as trauma or abuse, and a family history of mental health problems.

Warning Signs
  • Eating or sleeping too much
  • Pulling away from people and usual activities
  • Having low or no energy
  • Feeling helpless or hopeless
  • Experiencing extreme mood swings
  • Hearing voices or believing things that are not true
  • Thinking or harming yourself or others
